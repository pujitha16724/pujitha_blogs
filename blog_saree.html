Sarees

Kanjivaram saree

A Kanjivaram saree is a traditional silk saree from the Kanchipuram region of Tamil Nadu, India, known for its vibrant colors, intricate designs, and heavy zari work, often featuring motifs inspired by nature and temple art.
Banarasi saree
Banaras Brocade Sarees are made of finely woven silk and decorated with intricate designs using zari; this ornamentation is what makes the sarees heavy. Their special characteristics are Mughal-inspired designs such as intricate floral and foliate motifs, such as kalga and bel.
Chikankari saree
Chikankari,a timeless and intricate embroidery style, has long been celebrated for its grace and finesse. Originating in the heart of Lucknow, this delicate craft adorns a range of garments, including the elegant Chikankari sarees, which captivated women for centuries.
Chanderi saree
Chanderi saris are produced from three kinds of fabric: pure silk, Chanderi cotton and silk cotton. Traditional coin, floral art, peacocks and modern geometric designs are woven into different Chanderi patterns.


Pochampally saree
Pochampally is a famous weaving center in Telangana, India, known for its Ikat fabrics. The Ikat technique is a centuries-old art form that involves tie-dyeing the warp and weft threads before weaving. The result is bold pattern that are often inspired by birds, trees etc.
Gadwal saree

Gadwal sarees are a traditional, handcrafted Indian garment that are known for their intricate zari work, rich colors, and lightweight weave. They are woven in the Jogulamba Gadwal district of Telangana.
Kasavu saree

A typical white or off-white garment with a gold or silver coloured border is considered traditional and is called Kerala saree or Kerala Mundu. Garments made with kasavu are long, rectangular pieces of cloth called the mundu.
Bandhani saree

Bandhani, one of the oldest known methods of tie-dyeing, is still widely practiced in western India today. The fabric is made by pinching very small portions of cloth and tying them by plucking the cloth with the fingernails into many tiny bindings
